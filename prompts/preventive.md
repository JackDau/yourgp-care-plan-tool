# RACGP Red Book Preventive Activities

## Source
RACGP Guidelines for Preventive Activities in General Practice (Red Book, 10th Edition, 2024)

## Age-Based Screening

### Cancer Screening

**Bowel Cancer**
- Age 50-74: Faecal immunochemical test (FIT) every 2 years
- National Bowel Cancer Screening Program

**Breast Cancer**
- Age 50-74: Mammogram every 2 years
- BreastScreen Australia

**Cervical Cancer**
- Age 25-74: Cervical Screening Test every 5 years
- National Cervical Screening Program

**Skin Cancer**
- No population screening recommended
- Opportunistic full-body skin examination for high-risk individuals

**Prostate Cancer**
- No population screening recommended
- Informed decision-making if patient requests PSA testing
- Discuss benefits and harms

### Cardiovascular Risk Assessment

**Who to Screen:**
- All adults 45-79 years (no known CVD)
- Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander people from 30 years
- People with diabetes from 35 years

**How Often:**
- Every 2 years if low risk
- More frequently if intermediate/high risk or risk factors change

**What to Assess:**
- Blood pressure
- Lipids (TC, LDL, HDL, TG)
- Smoking status
- Diabetes status
- Family history
- Use Aus CVD Risk Calculator

### Diabetes Screening

**Who to Screen:**
- Adults 40+ years: every 3 years
- Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander 18+ years: every 3 years
- High-risk groups: as clinically indicated

**High-Risk Groups:**
- Overweight/obese
- Family history
- Previous gestational diabetes
- PCOS
- Certain ethnic backgrounds
- History of CVD

**How:**
- Fasting glucose or HbA1c
- OGTT if borderline results

### Osteoporosis Screening

**Who to Screen:**
- Women 70+ years
- Men 70+ years with risk factors
- Younger if clinical risk factors present

**Risk Factors:**
- Previous fracture
- Long-term corticosteroid use
- Low body weight
- Family history of hip fracture
- Smoking, excess alcohol

**How:**
- DXA bone density scan

## Immunisation Schedule (Adults)

### All Adults
- Influenza: Annual (especially 65+, chronic conditions)
- COVID-19: As per current recommendations
- dTpa: Every 10 years (or with each pregnancy)

### Age 50+
- Herpes zoster (shingles): Shingrix (2 doses) from age 50

### Age 65+
- Pneumococcal: 13vPCV then 23vPPV (or as per schedule)

### Age 60+
- RSV: Consider vaccination

### High-Risk Groups
- Hepatitis B (healthcare workers, chronic disease)
- Hepatitis A (travellers, liver disease)
- Meningococcal (asplenia, immunocompromised)

## Lifestyle Risk Factor Assessment

### Smoking
- Ask about tobacco use at every visit
- Provide brief intervention (5As)
- Offer Quitline referral and pharmacotherapy

### Physical Activity
- Assess current activity levels
- Recommend 150-300 min moderate intensity/week
- Muscle strengthening 2x/week

### Nutrition
- Assess dietary patterns
- Recommend Australian Dietary Guidelines
- Refer to dietitian if needed

### Weight
- Calculate BMI at least annually
- Measure waist circumference
- Target: BMI 18.5-24.9, waist <94cm (M), <80cm (F)

## Mental Health Screening

### Depression
- Screen opportunistically in high-risk groups
- PHQ-2 as initial screen
- PHQ-9 for assessment

### Anxiety
- GAD-7 for assessment
- Consider in patients with unexplained physical symptoms

### Alcohol and Drug Use
- AUDIT-C for alcohol
- Consider drug use screening in relevant populations

## Falls Prevention (65+ years)

- Ask about falls in past 12 months
- Assess gait and balance (Timed Up and Go test)
- Medication review
- Vision check
- Home hazard assessment
- Exercise program referral

## Key Preventive Interventions by Condition

### For Patients with Diabetes
- Annual eye examination
- Annual foot examination
- HbA1c every 3-6 months
- Lipids and kidney function annually
- Blood pressure every visit

### For Patients with CVD
- Lipid monitoring 6-12 monthly
- Blood pressure every visit
- Diabetes screening
- Medication adherence review

### For Patients with CKD
- Cardiovascular risk management
- Diabetes screening
- Anaemia screening
- Bone health monitoring (advanced CKD)

### For Patients with COPD
- Annual influenza vaccination
- Pneumococcal vaccination
- Smoking cessation (ongoing)
- Pulmonary rehabilitation
