# Mental Health Care Plan Guidelines

## Source
RACGP General Practice Mental Health Standards Collaboration (GPMHSC) Guidelines

## Important Note
This is for chronic mental health conditions managed under GPCCMP (Item 965).
Separate GP Mental Health Treatment Plans (GPMHTP) exist for acute/focused psychological treatment under Better Access.

## Key Management Areas

### Assessment
- Validated screening tools:
  - Depression: PHQ-9
  - Anxiety: GAD-7
  - General distress: K10
- Safety assessment (suicidal ideation, self-harm)
- Functional impact on daily life
- Substance use assessment

### Diagnosis
- Major depressive disorder
- Generalised anxiety disorder
- Panic disorder
- Social anxiety disorder
- PTSD
- Bipolar disorder (requires specialist input)
- Adjustment disorders

### Treatment Options

**Psychological Therapy**
- First-line for mild-moderate depression/anxiety
- CBT, interpersonal therapy, problem-solving therapy
- Access via psychologist, mental health social worker, OT

**Pharmacotherapy**
- SSRIs/SNRIs first-line for moderate-severe depression/anxiety
- Start low, go slow
- Review at 2-4 weeks, then regularly
- Continue for 12 months after remission (first episode)
- Taper gradually when ceasing

**Lifestyle Interventions**
- Physical activity (strong evidence for depression)
- Sleep hygiene
- Stress management
- Social connection
- Reducing alcohol/substance use

### Self-Management Strategies
- Mood tracking
- Behavioural activation (scheduling pleasant activities)
- Relaxation techniques
- Sleep routine
- Physical exercise

## Typical Allied Health Referrals
- Psychologist - CBT, evidence-based therapies
- Mental health social worker - counselling, social support
- Mental health OT - functional rehabilitation, return to work
- Exercise physiologist - structured exercise for mental health
- Psychiatrist - complex cases, medication-resistant, bipolar, psychosis

## Patient Self-Management Goals (Examples)
- Attend psychological therapy sessions
- Take medication as prescribed
- Practise relaxation/mindfulness daily
- Maintain regular sleep routine
- Exercise 3-5 times per week
- Limit alcohol to within guidelines
- Stay connected with supportive people
- Use crisis plan if needed

## Safety Planning
For patients with suicidal ideation:
- Warning signs to watch for
- Coping strategies to use
- People to contact for support
- Professional crisis services (Lifeline 13 11 14, Beyond Blue 1300 22 4636)
- Safe environment (remove means)

## Review Frequency
- Initial phase: every 2-4 weeks
- Stable phase: every 1-3 months
- Medication changes: review within 2 weeks

## Red Flags
- Active suicidal ideation with plan
- Psychotic symptoms
- Severe functional impairment
- Risk to self or others
- Manic episode
